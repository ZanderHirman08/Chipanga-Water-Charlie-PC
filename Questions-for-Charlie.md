# Questions for Charlie

Send this to Charlie however's easiest (WhatsApp voice note, email, whatever) — doesn't need to come
back as tidy prose, just needs his actual words/numbers and I'll fold them into the site.

## The story, in his words

1. Why did you start this project — what were you seeing in Chipanga before the well existed that made
   you want to take this on?
2. How did the Rotary connection happen — how did you end up working with Longmont Rotary and the
   wider Rotary network on this?
3. What's been the community's reaction so far — the women's garden group, the school, local leaders?
   Any specific moment that stuck with you?
4. What's the plan for after you leave — who runs/maintains this once your Peace Corps service ends?
5. If you could get one or two direct quotes from people involved (a woman from the garden group, a
   teacher, the headmaster/school board, a student) that we could put on the site as real voices, that'd
   be huge for investor trust. Even a sentence or two each.
6. What's the single most important thing you want an investor reading this site to understand or feel?
7. Rough timeline: when was the well dug, when do you expect solar + tanks + piping to be finished, when
   would the school garden reach full production?

## The school lunch / monthly-giving ask

This is the part that needs real numbers to be persuasive — right now it's just a good idea, and the
site can help make the case *if* we can show the math.

8. Once the school garden is fully running, what would it actually cost per month to keep it going and
   turn the harvest into lunches? (seeds/tool replacement, a cook's stipend, cooking fuel/charcoal, any
   ingredients you'd still need to buy beyond what the garden produces)
9. How many students would the lunch program reach, and how many days a week?
10. What specific monthly amount were you hoping the school board/parents would contribute, and what
    would that amount cover?
11. What has the actual pushback been from the school board/parents — is it that they don't believe the
    yield will be enough, that they don't trust the timeline, that it's just unfamiliar? Knowing the real
    objection helps target the messaging.
12. Do you know of any similar school-garden-to-lunch program elsewhere in Tanzania (or that you've heard
    of) with real cost/output numbers we could point to as precedent?

## Technical numbers (whatever you or the installer can confirm)

13. Solar array: panel wattage and how many panels, and the pump's power draw/model — I used your
    "5,000 L/hr max sustainable" figure as-is, but want to sanity check how much that drops in the
    rainy/cloudy season (I'm currently assuming ~6 effective pumping hours/day in dry season vs. ~4 in
    wet season — is that in the right ballpork, or does the installer/pump spec sheet say something more
    exact?)
14. Pipe diameter/material for the main lines (not required for the current water-budget numbers, but
    useful if we ever want to model actual flow/pressure).
15. Are the tank sizes in gallons US or Imperial? (8,000 US gal ≈ 30,300 L; 8,000 Imperial gal ≈ 36,400 L
    — matters for the liters figure on the site.)
16. Nearest weather station or region name you'd trust for climate data (is Chipanga closest to Singida,
    Dodoma, or somewhere else?) — right now the site's water-use numbers use generic semi-arid estimates,
    and real local rainfall/evapotranspiration data would make them much more credible.
17. Any local agricultural extension yield numbers for leafy greens, tomatoes, or okra in the area —
    the site currently uses generic smallholder estimates, real local numbers would be better.
